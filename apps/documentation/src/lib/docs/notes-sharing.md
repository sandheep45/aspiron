# Notes Sharing & Privacy

Aspiron provides granular control over who can see and access your notes.

## Privacy Levels

### Private (Default)

- Only visible to creator
- Not accessible to anyone else
- Fully personal
- Cannot be found in search

### Peer Shared

- Visible to specific peers you share with
- View-only access
- Cannot edit your notes
- Can comment (future)

### Public (Community)

- Visible to all students
- Read-only for community
- Attached to forum posts
- Can be searched

## Sharing Controls

### Sharing a Note

1. Open note
2. Click share icon
3. Select privacy level
4. Confirm

### Sharing with Peers

- Enter peer's username
- Send share request
- Peer accepts
- Note appears in their shared notes

### Making Public

- Select "Public" option
- Note becomes searchable
- Visible to all students
- Can be attached to forum posts

### Revoking Access

- Open note
- Change privacy level
- Access is revoked
- Notes disappear from shared views

## Community Notes

### What Can Be Shared

Students can share:

- Summary notes
- Important formulas
- Concept explanations
- Practice insights
- Study tips

### What Should NOT Be Shared

Students should not share:

- Full copied content
- Copyrighted material
- Incorrect information
- Spam or promotional content

### Quality Standards

Shared notes should be:

- Original content
- Accurate information
- Helpful to others
- Properly organized

## Forum Integration

### Attaching Notes

When posting in forum:

- Can attach your public notes
- References appear in post
- Others can view attached notes
- Credited to original creator

### Citation

- Notes show creator name
- Attribution preserved
- Cannot be claimed as own
- Builds community reputation

## Privacy Principles

### Student Control

Students always control:

- Who sees their notes
- What notes to share
- When to revoke access
- How notes are used

### Platform Commitment

Aspiron does NOT:

- Share notes without permission
- Sell note data
- Show notes publicly without consent
- Use notes for AI training (without consent)

### Data Protection

- Notes encrypted at rest
- Access logged for security
- Privacy violations investigated
- GDPR compliant (future)

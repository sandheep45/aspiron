# Revision Mode

Aspiron's revision mode provides a focused environment for reviewing mistakes and key content.

## What Is Revision Mode

### Definition

A dedicated mode for:

- Reviewing wrong answers
- Memorizing formulas
- Recalling key concepts
- Quick access to resources

### Access Points

Enter revision mode from:

- AI Recall Check results
- Wrong questions list
- Formula quick-reference
- AI suggestions
- Manual entry

## Revision Features

### Wrong Questions List

Shows all incorrectly answered questions:

- From practice quizzes
- From chapter tests
- From mock exams
- Organized by topic

Each question shows:
- Your answer
- Correct answer
- Explanation
- Link to related content

### Formula Review

Quick reference for formulas:

- By subject
- By chapter
- By topic
- Searchable

Features:
- Formula statements
- Variable meanings
- Example applications
- Related concepts

### Key Points Summary

AI-generated summaries:

- Important concepts
- Common mistakes
- Quick reminders
- Study tips

### AI Summaries

Combines:

- Teacher notes highlights
- Your notes summary
- Key formulas
- Important points

## Revision Workflow

### Identify Gaps

1. Open revision mode
2. Review wrong questions
3. Note patterns
4. Focus on weak areas

### Targeted Review

1. Click related video
2. Read relevant notes
3. Review formulas
4. Take follow-up quiz

### Track Improvement

1. Re-attempt wrong questions
2. See accuracy improvement
3. Build confidence
4. Reduce error rate

## Revision Tools

### Flashcards

- Formula flashcards
- Concept cards
- Custom cards (future)
- Spaced repetition (future)

### Quick Links

- Jump to video at timestamp
- Open related notes
- Start related quiz
- Ask AI for explanation

### Progress in Revision

- Questions mastered
- Topics reviewed
- Time spent
- Improvement over time

## Revision and Context

### Context-Aware

Revision mode shows:

- Relevant to your exam
- Filtered by your subject
- Targeted to your needs
- Aligned with your goals

### Personalized

AI personalizes revision:

- Focus on your weak areas
- Skip what you know
- Prioritize high-weight topics
- Balance coverage
